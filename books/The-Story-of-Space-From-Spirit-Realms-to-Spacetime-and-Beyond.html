Book Description

What is space? Is it emptiness, energy, geometry or something more?

The Story of Space is a sweeping and beautifully written exploration of how humanity has understood the cosmos across time, culture, and imagination. From ancient spirit realms to modern theories of spacetime, this book reveals how our concept of space has always been more than just science, it is a mirror of who we are.

Weaving together mythology, sacred geometry, Indigenous sky stories, psychological insight, language, science fiction, and cutting-edge physics, the book shows how space has evolved in human thought from a living presence to an abstract vacuum and now, perhaps, back again.

Drawing on history, art, culture, and intuitive speculation, this book challenges the idea that space is empty. Instead, it presents space as a vibrant, dynamic medium, one that might be alive, intelligent, or fundamentally connected to consciousness itself.

Accessible, poetic, and interdisciplinary, The Story of Space invites readers to rediscover the universe not only with their minds, but with their hearts and imagination. This book will reshape how you see the cosmos and your place within it.
